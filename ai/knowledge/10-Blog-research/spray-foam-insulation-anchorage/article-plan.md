### H2: Open Cell vs Closed Cell Spray Foam: Which is Best for Alaska? (500 words)

**H3: Understanding Closed Cell Spray Foam**
- R-value: R-6.5 to R-7 per inch
- Acts as vapor barrier
- Structural strengthening benefits
- Waterproof and moisture resistant
- Best for: exterior walls, attics, foundations, crawl spaces

**H3: Understanding Open Cell Spray Foam**
- R-value: R-3.5 to R-4 per inch
- Permeable to moisture (NOT a vapor barrier)
- Better soundproofing properties
- Lower cost per board foot
- Best for: interior walls, soundproofing applications

**H3: The Alaska Recommendation: Closed Cell**
- Why closed-cell is strongly recommended for Alaska
- CCHRC research on moisture management
- The 2/3 to 1/3 ratio for retrofits
- Real contractor perspectives

| Vapor barrier | Yes | No |
| Moisture resistance | Excellent | Poor |
| Cost per board foot | $1.20-$1.80 | $0.70-$1.10 |
| Alaska recommendation | Yes | Limited use |
