#### **FAQ 10: Do I need a vapor barrier with crawl space insulation?**

**Priority:** ðŸ”¥ MEDIUM-HIGH (Installation requirement)

**Brief Answer Outline:**
- YES - highly recommended in most cases
- Why vapor barriers matter:
  - Prevents ground moisture from rising
  - Protects insulation from dampness
  - Prevents mold and wood rot
  - Improves air quality
- Minimum: 6 mil polyethylene
- Recommended: 10-20 mil for durability
- Must cover:
  - Entire floor (overlapped and sealed)
  - Extended 6" up walls
- Exception: Only if on concrete slab with proper moisture barrier

#### **FAQ 12: How do I prevent mold in my insulated crawl space?**

**Priority:** ðŸ”¥ MEDIUM (Health concern, maintenance)

**Brief Answer Outline:**
- Prevention strategies:
  1. Install vapor barrier on ground
  2. Use moisture-resistant insulation (closed-cell spray foam or rigid foam)
  3. Seal all vents and air leaks
  4. Install dehumidifier (maintain 45-55% humidity)
  5. Fix water intrusion sources (gutters, grading, leaks)
  6. Regular inspections
- Avoid: Fiberglass batts (trap moisture)
- Warning signs:
  - Musty odors
  - Visible spots (black, green, white)
  - Increased allergies
  - Rising humidity
- Professional remediation needed if mold present

**Featured Snippet Opportunity:**

- Current ranking opportunity: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Include: Humidity target, material recommendations
