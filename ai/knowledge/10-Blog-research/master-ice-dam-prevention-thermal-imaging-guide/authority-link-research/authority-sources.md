### Key Quotes by Topic

**Topic 1: How Ice Dams Form**
> "An ice dam is a ridge of ice that forms at the edge of a roof and prevents melting snow (water) from draining off the roof. The water that backs up behind the dam can leak into a home and cause damage to walls, ceilings, insulation and other areas." - University of Minnesota Extension

> "Ice dams occur when warm air from your home's interior escapes into the attic, heating the underside of the roof. Ice dams occur when snow melts on a warm roof and the water refreezes across the eaves." - This Old House

> "Ice dam formation requires three things: snow on the roof, a poorly air sealed and/or poorly insulated attic, and freezing temperatures." - Building Science Corporation

**Topic 2: Air Sealing Priority**
> "Air sealing air highways from the living space below the attic is just as important as the insulation itself. Gaps around plumbing pipes and around chimneys can be a significant source of heat flow into the attic from the spaces below." - Insulation Institute

> "The first approach to preventing ice dams is 'air tightness, air tightness, air tightness.' While insulation is important, airtightness is most critical." - Building Science Corporation (Dr. Joseph Lstiburek)

> "The most important step is to seal all of the air leaks from the conditioned space into the attic space to keep warm air from entering the attic in the first place." - Building America Solution Center

**Topic 3: Insulation Requirements**
> "Most experts agree the R-value of attic insulation should be at least R-30 (R-38 is preferable in northern climates)." - National Weather Service

> "It is recommended that a minimum of R-30 should be provided below ventilated attic roof membranes, R-35 below ventilated cathedral ceilings, and R-40 below unventilated cathedral ceilings." - Building Science Corporation

> "Closed-cell spray foam insulation is applied in an attic space to achieve an R-value of 38, which is ideal for preventing heat loss and ice dam formation." - This Old House (Tom Silva)

**Topic 4: Ventilation Role**
> "In the winter, allowing a natural flow of outdoor air to ventilate the attic helps keep it cold, which reduces the potential for ice damming." - ENERGY STAR

> "Attic ventilation can reduce the likelihood of ice dam formation by cooling the roof deck." - Building America Solution Center

> "A ridge vent paired with continuous soffit vents circulates cold air under the entire roof." - This Old House

**Topic 5: Thermal Imaging Benefits**
> "Thermal imaging devices can find cold spots that suggest dampness, even if there's no ice dam on the roof at the time of the inspection." - Home Inspection Industry

> "The most accurate thermographic images usually occur when there is a large temperature difference (at least 20°F) between inside and outside air temperatures." - DOE Energy Saver

> "Insufficient insulation allows heat to escape, and infrared imaging offers a clear thermal map of poorly insulated ceiling and wall cavities that contribute to ice dams forming along the roof edges and walls." - Thermal Imaging Professionals

**Topic 6: Insurance and Cost Impact**
> "Damage caused by ice damming is one of the most common home insurance claims in the Northeast." - AAA Northeast

> "Water/ice damage makes up 20 percent of all homeowner insurance claims." - The Hanover Insurance

> "While the insurance company likely won't pay for the actual removal of the ice dam, they will typically cover the ensuing water damage to the home's interior." - American Family Insurance
