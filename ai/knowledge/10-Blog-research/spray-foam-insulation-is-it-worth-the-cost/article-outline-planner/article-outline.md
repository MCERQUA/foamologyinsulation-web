### Section 1: What Is Spray Foam Insulation? (300-350 words)
**H2:** What Is Spray Foam Insulation and How Does It Work?

**Key Points:**
- Two-component chemical mixture (isocyanates + polyol resin)
- Expands on contact to fill all gaps and cracks
- Two types: open-cell and closed-cell
- Professional installation required
- Creates airtight seal (unlike fiberglass)

**H3:** Open-Cell vs Closed-Cell Spray Foam

| Feature | Open-Cell | Closed-Cell |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| R-Value | 3.5-3.7/inch | 6.0-7.0/inch |
| Cost | $1.00-$3.00/sq ft | $1.50-$4.50/sq ft |
| Density | 0.5 lbs/cu ft | 1.75+ lbs/cu ft |
| Best For | Interior walls, soundproofing | Attics, crawl spaces, moisture-prone |
| Vapor Barrier | No | Yes |

**Keywords:** open cell vs closed cell, R-value, polyurethane foam

### Section 6: Potential Drawbacks and Concerns (400-450 words)
**H2:** Spray Foam Disadvantages: What You Need to Know

**Key Points:**
- Higher upfront cost (2-8x traditional insulation)
- Professional installation required
- Ventilation considerations for tight homes
- Difficulty accessing wiring/plumbing later
- Proper installation critical

**H3:** Safety Considerations
- 24-72 hour re-entry time during curing
- Isocyanate exposure during installation (workers only)
- Proper installation prevents issues

**H3:** Common Problems (And How to Avoid Them)
1. Off-ratio mixing (hire experienced contractors)
2. Improper thickness (multiple thin passes)
3. Moisture trapping (ensure dry surfaces)
4. Odor issues (verify materials, proper temp)
5. Over-expansion (experienced installers)

**H3:** When Spray Foam May NOT Be Worth It
- Planning to sell home soon (<5 years)
- Very tight budget
- Simple, small projects
- Contractor minimums exceed project scope

**Sources:** EPA Safety Guidelines, RetroFoam common problems guide
