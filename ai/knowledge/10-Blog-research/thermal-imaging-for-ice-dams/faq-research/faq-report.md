## Executive Summary

This comprehensive FAQ research covers 50+ questions across five major themes: ice dam causes and formation, thermal imaging technology, prevention methods, costs and ROI, and technical specifications. The research draws from Google PAA results, home improvement forums, Quora discussions, and professional sources.

## CATEGORY 1: ICE DAM FUNDAMENTALS

### High Priority Questions (Direct Target Keyword Match)

#### 1. What causes ice dams on roofs?
- **Source:** Google PAA (multiple sources)
- **Search Intent:** Informational - Understanding the problem
- **Recommended Length:** Medium (200-300 words)
- **Priority:** HIGH - Core topic
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Heat escapes from home into attic
  - Melts snow on upper roof (above 32°F)
  - Water flows to cold eaves (below 32°F)
  - Refreezes and forms ice dam ridge
  - Water backs up behind dam and leaks inside
- **Featured Snippet Potential:** YES
- **Sources:** [UMN Extension](https://extension.umn.edu/protecting-home-rain-and-ice/dealing-and-preventing-ice-dams), [Weather.gov](https://www.weather.gov/grr/roofIceDams)

#### 2. How do you prevent ice dams?
- **Source:** Google PAA
- **Search Intent:** Solution-focused
- **Recommended Length:** Detailed (400-500 words)
- **Priority:** HIGH - Primary conversion question
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Air seal attic bypasses (most important)
  - Add insulation (R-49 to R-60 recommended)
  - Improve ventilation (ridge + soffit vents)
  - Remove snow with roof rake
  - Install heated cables (temporary solution)
- **Featured Snippet Potential:** YES - List format
- **Sources:** [This Old House](https://www.thisoldhouse.com/roofing/21017362/how-to-prevent-ice-dams), [Clean Energy Resource Teams](https://www.cleanenergyresourceteams.org/story/got-ice-dams-what-do-and-how-prevent-them)

#### 3. What temperature causes ice dams to form?
- **Source:** Technical forums and building science sources
- **Search Intent:** Technical understanding
- **Recommended Length:** Brief (150-200 words)
- **Priority:** HIGH - Technical detail searchers
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Attic temperature above 30°F
  - Outdoor temperature below 22°F
  - 10°F differential along roof surface enables thaw-freeze
  - Upper roof surface above 32°F, eaves below 32°F
- **Featured Snippet Potential:** YES - Definition format
- **Sources:** [Building Science](https://buildingscience.com/documents/digests/bsd-135-ice-dams), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_dam_(roof))

#### 4. Does spray foam insulation prevent ice dams?
- **Source:** Google PAA, industry sources
- **Search Intent:** Commercial - Evaluating specific solution
- **Recommended Length:** Medium (250-350 words)
- **Priority:** HIGH - Direct conversion opportunity
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Yes, closed-cell spray foam highly effective
  - Creates both air barrier and thermal barrier
  - R-7 per inch (vs. R-3.6 for open-cell)
  - Seals air leaks while insulating
  - Best when applied to roof deck (unvented attic)
  - Must be professionally installed
- **Featured Snippet Potential:** YES
- **Sources:** [RetroFoam](https://www.retrofoamofmichigan.com/blog/spray-foam-insulation-prevent-ice-dams), [Spray Foam Magazine](https://www.sprayfoammagazine.com/foam-news/ice-dams-be-damned/3392)

## CATEGORY 2: THERMAL IMAGING TECHNOLOGY

### High Priority Questions

#### 9. Can thermal imaging detect insulation problems?
- **Source:** Google PAA
- **Search Intent:** Technology capability
- **Recommended Length:** Medium (200-250 words)
- **Priority:** HIGH - Core article topic
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Yes, highly effective
  - Shows hot/cold spots on walls and ceilings
  - Detects missing or settled insulation
  - Reveals air leaks around outlets, switches
  - Non-invasive (no drilling or removal needed)
  - Requires 15°F+ temperature differential
- **Featured Snippet Potential:** YES

- **Source:** Google PAA, technical sources
- **Search Intent:** Technical understanding
- **Recommended Length:** Medium (250-300 words)
- **Priority:** HIGH - Article foundation
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Infrared cameras detect heat radiation
  - Shows thermal map of ceiling/wall cavities
  - Reveals heat escaping through insulation gaps
  - Identifies cold air infiltration (shown in blue)
  - Can detect moisture/dampness from existing ice dams
  - Doesn't require attic access
  - Works best with 15°F+ temperature difference
- **Featured Snippet Potential:** YES
- **Sources:** [Massachusetts Thermal Imaging](https://massinfrared.com/infrared-applications/ice-dams/), [Fox MT](https://foxmt.com/blog-ice-dams/)

- **Priority:** HIGH - Clarification needed
- **Key Answer Points:**
  - Thermal cameras don't directly see ice dams on roof
  - They see heat loss CAUSING ice dams
  - Can detect water damage RESULTING from ice dams
  - Shows poorly insulated areas
  - Reveals cold spots from improper ventilation
  - Identifies dampness even without visible ice dam
- **Sources:** [InterNACHI Forum](https://forum.nachi.org/t/ice-dams-and-infrared-thermal-imaging/36835), [Ice Dam Company](https://www.icedamcompany.com/ice-dam-prevention/ice-dam-damage-inspection/)
