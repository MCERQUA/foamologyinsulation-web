## Insulation Demand Analysis

### Market Size Estimate
- **Total addressable market:** $6-8 million annually
- **Spray foam segment:** $2-3 million annually
- **Average project value:** $4,000-$14,000 residential
- **Growth premium:** Fastest-growing borough in Alaska

### Growth Trends
1. **New Construction:** 600-900 new homes annually (highest in state)
2. **Remodels/Retrofits:** 2,000+ insulation upgrades per year
3. **Second homes:** Many Anchorage residents building valley properties
4. **Agricultural buildings:** Barns, shops, greenhouses need insulation

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Peak Season:** May-September (shorter construction season)
- **Planning Season:** February-April (quotes and scheduling)
- **Emergency Season:** October-November (early cold snaps)
- **Shop/Garage Season:** Year-round (enclosed buildings)

### Economic Indicators
- Highest population growth in Alaska
- New subdivision developments ongoing
- Lower land costs attract builders
- Strong DIY culture (but pro work preferred for spray foam)

### Energy Cost Drivers
- **Heating oil:** $4.75-$5.75/gallon (delivery costs higher)
- **Propane:** Widely used, expensive
- **Electricity:** $0.24-$0.30/kWh
- **Wood heat:** Common supplemental heating
- **Annual heating costs:** $3,500-$7,000 typical home

### Competitive Gap Analysis
